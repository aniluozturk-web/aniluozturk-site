About.

A short biographical sketch.

PositionPostdoctoral ResearcherInstitutionFaculty of Law, Lund UniversityPronounshe/himLanguagesTurkish · English · French · Swedish (A1)

Since August 2025 I have been a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Faculty of Law, Lund University, working on the legal governance of CO2 transport and sub-seabed storage within a project funded by the Marcus and Marianne Wallenberg Foundation (PI: Olena Bokareva; Co-I: Gabriela Argüello, University of Gothenburg). I am also coordinating CCS at Sea 2026, an international conference at the Faculty in May 2026.

I hold a Ph.D. in Law from Maynooth University (Ireland, 2023), an LL.M. in International and Comparative Law from Trinity College Dublin (2017, First Class Honours with Distinction), and a B.A. in Law from Bilkent University (Ankara, 2016, High Honour). My doctoral thesis, The Almost People, addresses anthropomorphism, liability, and legal personhood in the age of social robots, supervised by Brian Flanagan.

My method is doctrinal-comparative, with a civil-law training and a preference for problems that move between jurisdictions.
